Draft Summary

Participant became distressed during a community outing due to unexpected loud noises. This escalated to physical aggression towards staff. Situation was de-escalated after moving to a quieter area.

NDIS Narrative Details

What Happened? expand_more
While walking near the construction site at the park, sudden drilling noise occurred. Participant covered ears and began shouting. Staff attempted to guide away, but participant struck out with open hand.
Lead Up & Triggers expand_more
Participant had a poor sleep the night prior (reported by night shift). Was already displaying signs of anxiety (pacing) before leaving the house. The specific trigger was sudden loud auditory input.
During Incident expand_more
Aggression lasted approx 2 minutes. No public involved. Staff maintained safe distance while ensuring participant safety. Verbal de-escalation scripts from BSP were used.
Response Actions expand_more
Staff removed participant from the noise source immediately. Offered water and a sensory toy once in a quiet zone. Transport called to return home early.
Causes & Contributing Factors expand_more
Primary cause: Sensory overload (Auditory). Secondary: Fatigue from lack of sleep.
